# Research Plan: Subpart F Analysis for Asian Trading Operations

**Date:** 2024-11-12
**Question:** Whether income from commodity trading and hedging services constitutes Subpart F income?

---

- **Short Title:** Asian Trading Subpart F Analysis
- **Question to Answer:** Whether [Cargill Entity A]'s commodity trading and hedging services income constitutes Subpart F income under IRC § 954
- **Jurisdictions / Regimes:** US Subpart F (IRC §§ 951-965); Hong Kong local tax
- **Time Period / Tax Years:** 2024 tax year
- **Deliverable:** Executive answer + Practitioner memo

## 2) Facts (Sanitized)

- [U.S. Parent] owns 100% of [Cargill Entity A], a CFC
- [Cargill Entity A] earned [Amount] from commodity trading in Southeast Asia (2024)
- [Amount] from sales to unrelated third parties (Singapore, Thailand, Vietnam)
- [Amount] from hedging services to affiliated entities
- Business conducted with local employees in Asia

---

## 3) Issues & Sub-Issues

1. **Foreign Base Company Sales Income (FBCSI)**
   - Hypothesis: If [Cargill Entity A] meets active conduct exception, trading income not FBCSI
   - What would prove: Documentation of substantial local activities and operational authority

2. **Foreign Base Company Services Income (FBCSvI)**
   - Hypothesis: Hedging services to affiliates are FBCSvI unless connected to CFC's own business
   - What would refute: Evidence services primarily support CFC's trading, not standalone affiliate services

3. **Active Conduct Exception Requirements**
   - Hypothesis: CFC has sufficient local operations to meet substantial activities test
   - What would prove: Employee count, functions, decision-making authority documentation
