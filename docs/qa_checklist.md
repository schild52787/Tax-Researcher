
# QA Checklist — International Tax Memos (Cargill)

> Use this checklist immediately before delivery. If any box is unchecked, fix or call it out under **Open Items**.

---

## A. Scope & intake
- [ ] Scope, jurisdictions, and question restated at top of memo.
- [ ] Facts sanitized (no client identifiers).
- [ ] Deliverable type confirmed (Exec answer + Practitioner memo).
- [ ] Deadline met; version/date set in header.

## B. Research plan & sources
- [ ] Research plan created and approved (primary before secondary).
- [ ] All **primary authorities** identified for each sub‑issue.
- [ ] Secondary sources (if any) are **flagged as secondary**.
- [ ] Key authorities are **current** (no repeals/obsolescence noted).

## C. Citations (form & substance)
- [ ] **Statutes/Regs** cited with precise sections (e.g., IRC § 951A(c)(2)(A)(i); Treas. Reg. § 1.951A‑2(b)(2)(i)).
- [ ] **Cases** use **Bluebook** form with **pincites**; court & year included.
- [ ] **Treaties/TEs** include article numbers (LOB/BO where relevant) and pins.
- [ ] **OECD** Model/Commentary and Pillar Two **Administrative Guidance** include article/paragraph/section pins.
- [ ] **Local law** cites follow local convention or accepted shorthand; link to official source when possible.
- [ ] **Public links** provided where available (IRS.gov, OECD.org, EUR‑Lex, official gazettes); **date accessed** recorded.
- [ ] Web sources **archived** or saved “on file.”
- [ ] Any uncertainty labeled **“Unknown—needs manual check.”**
- [ ] No fabricated citations.

## D. Case validation (mandatory for material cases)
- [ ] Shepard’s (Lexis) or BCite (Bloomberg Law) run for each material case.
- [ ] Negative/limiting treatment evaluated; subsequent history reflected in the cite as needed.
- [ ] Evidence of check retained (screenshot or note “on file”).

## E. Law → facts mapping
- [ ] Each conclusion explicitly ties authority to the fact pattern.
- [ ] Definitions and treaty terms (beneficial owner, PE, etc.) applied correctly.
- [ ] Assumptions are listed and **justified**.
- [ ] **Follow‑up questions** included.

## F. Red‑team & risk
- [ ] **Three** counter‑arguments presented with authority and **likelihood** (Low/Med/High).
- [ ] **Overall risk rating** assigned on the **opinion scale**: Reasonable authority / Substantial authority / More likely than not / Should.
- [ ] **Risk & Penalty Shield** section **included only if** overall risk > Medium and tied to documentation (reasonable cause, substantial authority, contemporaneous records).

## G. Data & calculations (if any)
- [ ] Formulas explained in prose; results reconcile to sources.
- [ ] Rounding/assumptions stated; currency codes used (USD/EUR/etc.).
- [ ] Pillar Two / ETR computations include safe‑harbour checks; data gaps noted.

## H. Formatting & consistency
- [ ] Required sections present (Exec Answer; Issue; Facts; Law & Authorities; Analysis; Conclusion; Red‑Team; (Risk section if > Medium); Follow‑Ups; Exhibits).
- [ ] Executive Answer ≤150 words and stands alone.
- [ ] Headings and tables match **House Style**.
- [ ] Exhibits labeled (Ex. A, B, …) with descriptors.
- [ ] Cross‑references (e.g., “See §4.2, infra.”) correct and linked where possible.

## I. Privacy, security & admin
- [ ] Redactions verified; placeholders used for identifiers.
- [ ] File name follows convention `YYYY‑MM‑DD_Country_Matter_Deliverable_v##`.
- [ ] Confidentiality footer included if external circulation is possible.
- [ ] **AI usage compliant:** only enterprise‑approved tools used; sensitive facts anonymized.
